# Response feedback
{: #feedback}

 

After you receive a response to your question in **Conversations**, you can provide feedback on the answer to help improve future responses to similar questions. This feedback feature is available in {{site.data.keyword.wxbia_short}} as a Service only.
{: #shortdesc}

Feedback is collected at the project level. Data analysts who have access to the project review the feedback. 

Since this feature supports team collaboration, each project needs to have an active **Admin** or **Editor** who can view and act on the feedback received for data in the project. 


## Providing feedback
{: #provide_feedback}

After you receive an answer, you can provide feedback directly in the conversation.

1. Review the generated answer.

1. Select Thumbs up (**I like this response**) to provide positive feedback or Thumbs down (**I dislike this response**) to flag the response for inaccuracies. You can find these icons under the response.

1. Select a feedback category and enter additional details. Focus on the business context, such as expected results, definitions, or time ranges.

  Avoid adding confidential or personal information in the feedback. 
  {: important}

1. Click **Submit**.

After submission, your feedback is recorded and becomes visible to Data analysts.

Feedback on a response that does not use metrics is visible to all Data analysts and Administrators. For example, feedback to responses for questions such as, *What data do I have available to me?* can be viewed by all Data analysts and Administrators.
{: note}

### How your feedback is used
{: #feedback_use}

Submitting feedback does not automatically change answers generated by the LLM. Data analysts use feedback to:

- Improve semantic data models

- Clarify or redefine metrics

- Adjust AI instructions based on how questions are phrased

- Identify gaps in data 

Improvements are applied at the project level and can affect future responses.


## Reviewing feedback (Data analysts)
{: #review_feedback}

As a Data analyst, you can view feedback in the **Feedback center** across all projects that you have **Admin** or **Editor** access to. 

Feedback is shown in a list view that makes it easy to scan and prioritize issues. 

For each feedback item, you can view:

- Feedback type and category
- Feedback author
- Submission date and time
- The original question
- Assets used to answer the question 
- Feedback status

You can download this list view of feedback as a CSV file. You can also filter feedback to focus on relevant or high-priority items.

### Detailed view of feedback
{: #detailed_feedback}

Select a feedback item to open its detailed view. Use this view to understand why a response was helpful or why it was inaccurate.

In addition to the list view information, the detailed view includes:

- The generated response and any associated visualization

- The steps the AI used to interpret and answer the question

You can download components of the individual feedback locally. The downloaded file includes the text response and any visualization that was generated.

### Managing feedback
{: #manage_feedback}

New feedback appears in the **Feedback center** with a **New** status. When a new feedback item is opened for the first time, the status changes to **Open**.

Upon review and resolution, Data analysts can mark items as **Closed**. You can also reopen closed feedback should it require further improvements. 

Feedback cannot be deleted from the **Feedback center**. 


### Limitations
{: #limitations_feedback}

- Analytics consumers cannot view the **Feedback center** to track the progress of their feedback.

- Deleting a project or metric can orphan related feedback. Orphaned feedback remains in the system but becomes inaccessible because it no longer links to an available project or metric.

- Users with the Data analyst or Administrator role who only have Viewer access to a project can see feedback but might not be able to take action on it.
